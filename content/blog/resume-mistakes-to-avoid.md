Most rejections are not about your qualifications — they are about avoidable **resume mistakes** that quietly knock you out before anyone reads your achievements. A broken layout, a vague summary, or a single typo can end your application in seconds. The encouraging part: every one of these errors is fixable in an afternoon. This guide walks through the 12 most common resume mistakes that get CVs rejected in 2026 and gives you the resume tips to fix each one.

If you have been applying for weeks with no callbacks, the problem is probably hiding in this list.

## Why Small Mistakes Cause Big Rejections

In India's high-volume hiring, a single role can draw hundreds of applications. Recruiters spend 6–10 seconds on the first scan, and Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) auto-filter before that. In this environment, small resume errors carry outsized cost — one parsing failure or one weak opening line ends the application. Understanding this is the first step; for the recruiter's-eye view, read [why resume rejected](/blog/why-resume-rejected).

## Mistake 1: A Layout That Breaks ATS

The most damaging error is invisible to you. Two-column templates, tables, text boxes, sidebars, and graphics often scramble when software parses them, so your content arrives as garbage — or not at all.

**Fix:** Use a single-column layout, standard fonts, and no tables or text boxes. Our [ATS-friendly resume](/blog/ats-friendly-resume) guide has the full checklist, and [simple resume format](/blog/simple-resume-format) shows a safe structure.

## Mistake 2: A Generic Objective Instead of a Summary

"Seeking a challenging position to utilize my skills and grow with the organization" tells a recruiter nothing. It is filler that wastes your most-read lines.

**Fix:** Replace it with a 2–3 line summary stating your target role and one strong proof point. See [resume summary examples](/blog/resume-summary-examples-india).

## Mistake 3: Duty-Focused Bullets With No Results

"Responsible for managing the team" describes your job description, not your impact. Recruiters already know the duties — they want outcomes.

**Fix:** Rewrite bullets using action verb + task + result. "Led a 5-member team to deliver the project 1 week early." Our [work experience resume section](/blog/work-experience-resume-section) and [resume action verbs](/blog/resume-action-verbs) guides show exactly how.

## Mistake 4: No Numbers Anywhere

A resume with zero metrics reads like everyone else's. Numbers create credibility and contrast.

**Fix:** Quantify scale, time, money, or quality. "Handled 40+ client accounts," "saved 6 hours/week," "reduced errors by 30%." Use honest estimates if exact figures are unavailable — never fabricate.

## Mistake 5: Typos and Inconsistent Formatting

A single spelling error — especially in the company name or your own job title — signals carelessness. Inconsistent fonts, dates, and bullet styles compound the impression.

**Fix:** Proofread slowly, read it aloud, and ask someone else to check. Keep fonts, date formats, and spacing consistent throughout.

## Mistake 6: A Skills Section Full of Filler

"Hardworking, dedicated, team player" appears on millions of resumes and persuades no one. Star ratings and percentage bars are equally meaningless.

**Fix:** List specific, relevant, defensible skills grouped by category. Prove soft skills in your experience bullets instead. See [resume skills section](/blog/resume-skills-section).

## Mistake 7: Too Long (or Padded)

A two-page resume for a fresher with one internship signals padding. So does a tiny font crammed to fit everything.

**Fix:** One page for freshers and most candidates under ~7 years of experience. Cut the irrelevant and keep the strong. For length specifics, our [work experience resume section](/blog/work-experience-resume-section) guide helps you trim.

## Mistake 8: Outdated Personal Details

Photos, date of birth, marital status, father's name, full home address, and a "Declaration" line are leftovers from an older era. They waste space and, in the case of photos, are rejected by many Indian IT firms.

**Fix:** Include city (not full address), phone, professional email, and LinkedIn. Drop the rest.

## Mistake 9: An Unprofessional Email Address

`coolboy_99@...` or `princessjaan@...` undermines an otherwise solid resume in a single line.

**Fix:** Use a simple `firstname.lastname@` style address. It costs nothing and removes an easy reason to doubt you.

## Mistake 10: Sending the Same Resume Everywhere

A generic CV blasted to fifty roles ranks low on keyword match and feels irrelevant to each one.

**Fix:** Keep one master resume and tailor lightly per role — adjust the summary, reorder skills, and swap the top bullets to match the job description honestly.

## Mistake 11: Vague Projects (Freshers)

"Made a website for college project" tells a recruiter nothing about your ability.

**Fix:** Describe projects with tech stack and outcomes. "Built a college event portal (HTML, CSS, JS) used by 400+ students." Our [student resume guide](/blog/student-resume-guide) and [resume with no experience](/blog/resume-with-no-experience) guides show how to make projects shine.

## Mistake 12: Broken Links and Wrong Files

A 404 GitHub link, a dead portfolio URL, or sending a .docx when the portal demands a PDF (or vice versa) can end an application instantly.

**Fix:** Test every link before sending. Confirm the required file format and name it `YourName_Resume.pdf`. Open the final file on your phone to confirm it renders correctly.

## A Quick Pre-Submit Checklist

Before you hit apply, run through this:

1. Single-column, ATS-safe layout
2. Summary with a target role and proof point
3. Impact bullets with numbers
4. Zero typos; consistent formatting
5. Relevant, grouped, defensible skills
6. One page (unless genuinely justified)
7. No photo/DOB/declaration; professional email
8. Tailored to the specific role
9. All links tested and working
10. Correct file format and name

## FAQ

### What is the most common resume mistake?

A layout that breaks ATS parsing — two-column templates, tables, and graphics. Your content may never reach a human, no matter how strong it is.

### Why does my resume keep getting rejected?

Usually a combination of these errors: ATS-unfriendly formatting, duty-focused bullets with no results, a generic summary, or sending the same untailored CV everywhere. See [why resume rejected](/blog/why-resume-rejected) for the recruiter's perspective.

### How do I know if my resume has these mistakes?

Run it through an automated review. An AI roast flags weak bullets, ATS risks, and filler in seconds — far faster than guessing.

### Should I remove my photo from my resume?

For most roles in India, yes. Many IT and service companies reject photos, and they waste valuable space. Use that space for content instead.
