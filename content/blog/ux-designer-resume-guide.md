A winning **ux designer resume** has to do something ironic: it must demonstrate good design and great communication on a single page. If your own CV is cluttered, hard to scan, or full of vague claims, no recruiter will believe you can design clean, user-friendly products. For UX and UI UX designer roles in India — at startups, product companies, and agencies — your resume and portfolio work as a pair. This guide shows you how to make both convince a hiring manager fast.

The biggest mistake designers make? Treating the resume as an afterthought to the portfolio. Recruiters see the resume first. If it does not earn a click to your portfolio, the portfolio never gets seen.

## What Hiring Managers Look For in a UI UX Resume

For UX designer jobs, reviewers scan for:

- **A portfolio link that works** (this is non-negotiable)
- **Process evidence** — research, wireframes, testing, iteration, not just pretty screens
- **Impact** — how your design changed a metric or solved a real user problem
- **Tool fluency** — Figma above all in 2026

Junior designers should prove they understand the full UX process, not just visual design. Senior designers should show ownership, leadership, and business impact.

## Key Skills to Feature on a UX Designer Resume

Group skills so they are easy to scan and ATS-friendly. Lead with what the role needs.

### Core UX/UI skills

- **Design tools:** Figma, Adobe XD, Sketch
- **UX process:** user research, personas, journey mapping, information architecture
- **Wireframing & prototyping:** low- and high-fidelity, interactive prototypes
- **Usability testing:** A/B testing, user interviews, heuristic evaluation
- **Visual/UI:** design systems, typography, colour theory, accessibility (WCAG)

### Supporting skills

- Interaction design and micro-interactions
- Basic HTML/CSS understanding (helps collaboration)
- Stakeholder communication and design handoff (Figma dev mode, Zeplin)
- Tools like Maze, Hotjar, or Google Analytics for insights

Do not list every Adobe app you have opened. A focused [resume skills section](/blog/resume-skills-section) reads as confidence; a 20-tool list reads as padding.

## UX Designer Resume Summary Example

Two or three lines: who you are, your strongest proof, and your focus. Avoid generic "creative problem solver" lines.

**For a junior / fresher:**

> UI/UX designer with a strong Figma portfolio and 4 end-to-end case studies, including a redesign that improved task completion by 30% in usability tests. Skilled in user research, wireframing, and prototyping, seeking a junior UX role in a product team.

**For 2+ years experience:**

> Product designer with 3 years designing mobile-first fintech experiences. Led a checkout redesign that lifted conversion by 12% and built a reusable design system adopted across 4 squads. Strong in research-driven design and cross-functional collaboration.

Both anchor on outcomes. For more structures, see our [resume summary examples for India](/blog/resume-summary-examples-india).

## Strong Bullet Points (With Metrics)

Designers love visuals, but bullets still need to prove impact. Use Problem → Action → Result.

1. **Redesigned** the onboarding flow based on user interviews, reducing drop-off by 25% and improving Day-1 activation.
2. **Conducted** 15 usability tests that surfaced a navigation issue, leading to a redesign that cut support tickets by 18%.
3. **Built** a Figma design system of 60+ components, cutting design-to-dev handoff time by 30%.
4. **Created** wireframes and prototypes for a new feature, validated with 12 users before a single line of code was written.
5. **Improved** accessibility to WCAG AA, increasing usable contrast across 40+ screens and broadening the app's reach.

If you are early-career, quantify your case studies: number of users tested, screens designed, iterations made. Avoid inventing conversion numbers you cannot back up. Strengthen your phrasing with our [resume action verbs](/blog/resume-action-verbs).

## The UX Designer Portfolio (Your Real Resume)

For UI UX roles, the portfolio decides everything. Your resume's main job is to earn the click.

### Portfolio essentials

- **A clear, working link** at the top of your resume (custom domain or Behance/Notion)
- **3–4 deep case studies** over 10 shallow ones
- **Show process:** problem, research, ideation, design, testing, outcome
- **Tell the story:** why you made each decision, not just the final screens

A ux designer portfolio without process is just a gallery. Recruiters want to see how you think. Briefly reference one signature project on the resume and link to the full case study.

## Common UI UX Resume Mistakes

These quietly sink designer applications:

- **No portfolio link**, or a broken/private one. Instant reject.
- **Over-designed resume** with custom fonts and columns that break ATS and distract.
- **Only showing final UI** with no research or testing evidence.
- **Vague claims:** "improved user experience" with no metric or method.
- **Listing tools without context** about how you used them.
- **Burying the portfolio link** at the bottom in tiny text.

## ATS Keyword Tips for UX Designers

Yes, even design roles often pass through ATS at larger Indian companies. To get through:

- **Keep the resume itself clean and parseable** — single column, standard fonts. Save the visual flair for the portfolio.
- **Mirror the job description:** "user research," "wireframing," "prototyping," "Figma," "design systems," "usability testing."
- **Spell out terms once:** "User Experience (UX)," "User Interface (UI)."
- **Include role keywords:** interaction design, information architecture, user-centered design, accessibility, A/B testing.
- **Export as PDF** with selectable text (not an image of your resume).

## UI vs UX vs Product Designer (Position Yourself Right)

Indian job titles blur these roles, so target the right keywords:

- **UI designer:** visual design, design systems, typography, components.
- **UX designer:** research, journeys, testing, problem-solving.
- **UI/UX designer:** both, common at startups — show range.
- **Product designer:** UX plus business and product thinking, metrics ownership.

Read each job description and adjust your summary and skill order to match. A startup wanting a generalist needs different framing than an enterprise wanting a research specialist.

## Resume Structure That Works

A clean one-page order for UX designer jobs:

1. **Header** — name, city, email, phone, portfolio link, LinkedIn
2. **Summary** — 2–3 lines with a metric
3. **Skills** — grouped, role-matched
4. **Experience** — impact bullets, one linked signature project
5. **Selected Projects / Case Studies** — for juniors, place above experience
6. **Education** — degree, relevant courses, year

## Final Pre-Submit Checklist

Before you apply:

- Is the portfolio link at the top and working?

- Does the summary name the exact role (UI, UX, or product)?
- Is the resume itself clean, single-column, and ATS-safe?
- Have you tailored skills to this specific job description?
