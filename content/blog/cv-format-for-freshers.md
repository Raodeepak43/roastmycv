Writing a **cv for freshers** feels impossible when your work-experience section is basically empty — and that panic leads to two equally bad outcomes: a CV padded with fluff, or a CV so bare it looks unfinished. Here is the good news: recruiters hiring freshers in India do not expect years of experience. They expect a clean, well-structured document that proves you can learn, communicate, and contribute. This guide gives you the exact CV format for freshers that works in the Indian job market, what to put in every section when you have no experience, and how to make it stand out among the 500 applications flooding every campus role.

## What Recruiters Actually Look for in a Fresher CV

Before formatting anything, understand the mindset of the person reading it. For a fresher, recruiters scan for:

- **Relevant skills** they can use from day one or train quickly
- **Projects and internships** that show applied learning
- **Clear academics** (degree, branch, CGPA, graduation year)
- **Communication** — a clean, error-free CV signals attention to detail
- **ATS compatibility** — at large firms, a parser screens you first

You are not competing on years served. You are competing on clarity and potential. A messy CV from a brilliant candidate loses to a clean CV from an average one. That is the uncomfortable truth.

## The Ideal CV Format for Freshers (Section by Section)

Here is the order that works best for a cv for freshers with no experience. Lead with your strengths, not your gaps.

### 1. Header and Contact Details

Keep it tight: full name, city, phone number, a professional email, and your LinkedIn URL. Skip your full home address, photo, age, and marital status — they waste space and add nothing.

### 2. Career Objective or Summary (2 Lines)

A short, targeted summary beats a generic objective. Instead of "Seeking a challenging role to utilize my skills," write something specific: "Computer Science fresher skilled in Python and SQL, seeking a data analyst role to apply academic projects in a real product team." Need ideas? Our [resume summary examples for India](/blog/resume-summary-examples-india) translate directly to fresher CVs.

### 3. Education

For freshers, education comes high up. Include:

- Degree, branch, and college name
- Graduation year (or expected year)
- CGPA or percentage if it is 7.0/70% or above
- Relevant coursework only if it strengthens your case

### 4. Projects (Your Secret Weapon)

This is where freshers win or lose. Projects replace work experience. For each, write:

- Project name and one-line purpose
- Tech stack or tools used
- Your specific contribution
- The outcome or result

Use the Action + Result format: "Built a college event registration site with React and Firebase used by 600+ students."

### 5. Internships and Training

Any internship, even unpaid or virtual, goes here. Describe what you *did*, not what the company does.

### 6. Skills

List 8–12 genuine skills you can defend in an interview. Group them (Languages, Tools, Soft Skills) for readability. Do not list "MS Office" as a headline skill in 2026.

### 7. Certifications, Achievements, Extracurriculars

Coding contest ranks, NPTEL certificates, club leadership, fest organizing — these show initiative. Keep them brief.

## How Long Should a Fresher CV Be?

One page. Full stop. A fresher with a two-page CV almost always has padding, repetition, or oversized fonts. If you are struggling to fill one page, your projects and skills sections need more substance, not bigger margins. If you are overflowing, you are probably listing duties instead of results.

## Formatting Rules That Keep You Out of the Reject Pile

The best content fails if the format is wrong. Stick to these:

1. **Single-column layout.** Two-column designs often scramble in ATS parsers.
2. **Standard fonts** — Calibri, Arial, or Georgia at 10–12pt.
3. **No photos, tables, or text boxes** for corporate roles.
4. **Consistent date formats** and bullet styles throughout.
5. **PDF export** unless the employer asks for Word.
6. **Clean file name** — "Firstname-Lastname-CV.pdf."

These are the same principles behind any [ATS-friendly resume](/blog/ats-friendly-resume), and they matter even more for freshers because campus drives run high volumes through automated filters first.

## Writing a CV for Freshers With No Experience

If you have zero internships and zero jobs, you are not stuck — you are just reframing. Pull material from:

- **Academic projects** (final year, mini-projects, hackathons)
- **Self-learning** (courses completed with a project to show for it)
- **Volunteer work** and college club roles
- **Freelance or personal projects** (a blog, an app, a design portfolio)
- **Competitions** (coding contests, case competitions, sports at a serious level)

The trick is to describe these with the same achievement language you would use for a job. "Volunteer" becomes "Coordinated logistics for a 300-person college tech fest." The work counts — you just have to frame it like it counts.

## Common Fresher CV Mistakes

Avoid the classics that get freshers filtered instantly:

- **Listing "team player" and "hard worker"** without proof. Show, do not tell.
- **One CV for every company.** Tailor at least your summary and skills order.
- **Burying projects** at the bottom where recruiters never reach.
- **Fake or exaggerated skills.** "Expert in Java" when you wrote two assignments is interview suicide.
- **Cluttered, multi-color designs** that look creative but break parsing.

## Fresher CV FAQ

### Should a fresher CV include a photo?

No, not for corporate or IT roles in India. Photos waste space and can introduce bias. Only include one if explicitly requested.

### What if I have no projects either?

Build one this week. A small personal project — a website, a data analysis, a simple app — gives you something concrete to talk about and instantly strengthens your CV.

### Is CGPA mandatory on a fresher CV?

Include it if it is 7.0/70% or higher. If it is lower, lead with strong projects and skills, and let those carry the document.

### Can I use the same CV for off-campus and on-campus?

Use the same base, but tailor the summary and skills order to each role. A generic CV underperforms a targeted one every time.

### How do I know if my fresher CV is good enough?

Test it. Run it through a free roast for an honest score and specific fixes — far more useful than a friend saying "looks fine."
