## Before You Start: Gather Your Material

Trying to create a resume online while hunting for details mid-build wastes time. First, collect:

- **Education** — degrees, college names, CGPA, years of passing, 10th/12th marks
- **Projects** — titles, tech stacks, and what each achieved
- **Experience / internships** — company names, durations, responsibilities
- **Skills** — only what you can defend in an interview
- **Links** — LinkedIn, GitHub, portfolio
- **Achievements** — certifications, hackathon ranks, awards

With this ready, building your resume online takes 20–30 minutes instead of an hour.

## Step 2: Pick a Clean Template

Choose a single-column, professional layout. Avoid:

- Two-column designs (they scramble in ATS parsing)
- Photos (often prohibited for IT roles in India)
- Heavy graphics and icons
- Decorative fonts

A clean template ensures both the software and the human can read your content. Browse options in our [resume templates guide](/blog/resume-templates-guide).

## Step 3: Build Your Contact Block

Keep it professional and minimal:

- Full name (as per official documents)
- Indian mobile number
- A professional email address
- LinkedIn URL (cleaned up)
- GitHub or portfolio for tech and design roles
- City and state only — no full address

## Step 4: Write a Sharp 2-Line Summary

Skip the outdated "career objective." Open with two lines stating your target role and strongest proof point. A fresher might write: "B.Tech CSE 2026 (CGPA 8.1) with two full-stack projects and a 3-month internship. Targeting backend developer roles." See more [resume summary examples for India](/blog/resume-summary-examples-india).

## Step 5: Add Sections in Priority Order

Order your sections by what is strongest for you:

1. **Education** — especially important for freshers
2. **Skills** — grouped logically, 8–12 core items
3. **Projects** — 2–3 strong ones with metrics
4. **Experience / internships** — even short ones count
5. **Certifications / achievements** — relevant only

## Step 6: Write Strong, Specific Bullets

This is where most resumes fail. Every bullet should follow action → what you did → measurable result.

- **Weak:** "Worked on a website for the college."
- **Strong:** "Built a responsive college events website (React, Firebase) used by 500+ students during fest week."

Use action verbs and numbers. Vague claims get ignored; specific results get interviews. For what to avoid, read [why resumes get rejected](/blog/why-resume-rejected).

## Step 7: Optimize for ATS

When you create resume online free, the template handles structure, but you handle keywords:

- Mirror the job description's wording honestly
- Place skills in context within bullets, not just a list
- Use standard section headings
- Avoid keyword stuffing

For the full ATS playbook, read our [ATS-friendly resume guide](/blog/ats-friendly-resume).

## Step 8: Export and Test

Download your resume as a PDF (unless a portal requests .docx). Then:

1. Open the PDF on both phone and laptop to check formatting
2. Confirm the text is selectable, not an image
3. Verify all links work

## Step 9: Get Honest Feedback Before You Apply

A resume you just built always looks better to you than it is. Before sending it to 50 companies, get an outside check. Our [free AI resume roast](/) reads your actual CV and flags weak bullets, missing metrics, and ATS issues — with a score out of 10 and specific fixes. Fix the top problems, then re-check.

## Step 10: Tailor Per Job

Keep your finished resume as a master version. For each application, adjust:

- The summary (two lines matching the role)
- Skills order (mirror the job description)
- Top two bullets under your most relevant experience or project

This light tailoring boosts your keyword match without rebuilding the whole CV each time.

## Common Mistakes When Making a Resume Online

- **Trusting the editor preview** instead of checking the exported PDF
- **Using a flashy template** that fails ATS
- **Listing filler skills** like "MS Office, Hard Working"
- **One generic resume** for every job
- **Skipping the feedback step** before applying
- **Broken or outdated links**
