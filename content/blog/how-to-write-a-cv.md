Learning **how to write a cv** from a blank page is intimidating — you stare at the cursor, unsure what to include, what to leave out, and what recruiters actually want to see. The truth is that writing a curriculum vitae is a repeatable process, not a creative gift. Follow the right steps in the right order and you will produce a clean, persuasive CV that survives ATS filters and earns the six-second recruiter scan. This guide walks you through cv how to write it from scratch — section by section, with examples geared to the Indian job market and a clear path from blank document to interview-ready.

## Before You Start: Gather Your Material

Do not open a template yet. First, collect your raw ingredients so you are not stalling mid-way:

- Contact details (city, phone, professional email, LinkedIn)
- Education — degrees, institutions, years, CGPA if 7.0+
- Work history, internships, and freelance projects with dates
- Projects with tools used and outcomes
- Skills you can genuinely defend
- Achievements, certifications, and awards with numbers where possible

Having this ready turns cv writing for job applications from a two-hour ordeal into a 30-minute task.

## Step 1: Choose the Right Structure

Pick a layout before you write. For most people in India, a reverse-chronological, single-column structure is the safest and most ATS-friendly choice. Your sections, in order:

1. Header and contact
2. Professional summary
3. Work experience (or projects, for freshers)
4. Skills
5. Education
6. Certifications and extras

Freshers should lead with projects and education; experienced candidates lead with work experience. If layout choices confuse you, our [CV layout examples](/blog/cv-layout-examples) guide compares the main structures side by side.

## Step 2: Write the Header

Keep it minimal and professional:

- Full name (largest text on the page)
- City and state (no full home address needed)
- Phone number and a professional email
- LinkedIn URL and portfolio link if relevant

Skip photos, date of birth, and marital status for corporate roles in India — they add nothing and waste space.

## Step 3: Write a Sharp Professional Summary

This is the most-read section, so make it count. In three or four lines, state who you are, your strongest proof point, and what you want. Compare:

Weak: "Hardworking individual seeking a challenging role to grow my career."

Strong: "Marketing graduate with internship experience growing a D2C brand's Instagram by 40%. Skilled in content strategy and analytics. Seeking a digital marketing executive role."

Specific beats generic every time. Our [resume summary examples for India](/blog/resume-summary-examples-india) give you templates you can adapt directly.

## Step 4: Write Achievement-Based Experience

This is the heart of writing a curriculum vitae, and where most CVs go wrong. The rule: **show impact, not duties.**

Use this three-part formula for each bullet:

1. **Action verb** — led, built, increased, reduced, launched
2. **What you did** — the task or project
3. **Result** — quantified wherever possible

### Sample: How to Write a CV Bullet

Weak: "Worked on the company website."

Strong: "Rebuilt the company website in React, cutting page load time 35% and lifting mobile conversions 12%."

Even without hard numbers, you can show scope: "Managed inventory across 3 warehouses" beats "Responsible for inventory."

## Step 5: Build a Relevant Skills Section

List 8–14 genuine skills, grouped for readability (Technical, Tools, Soft Skills). Match the language of the job description honestly — if they ask for "data visualization" and you have it, use that phrase. Avoid padding with obvious skills like "MS Word" or empty claims like "good communication" without proof.

## Step 6: Add Education and Extras

For experienced professionals, keep education brief — degree, institution, year. For freshers, expand it with CGPA and relevant coursework. Then add certifications, awards, and notable extracurriculars that strengthen your case.

## Step 7: Tailor It to the Job

A generic CV is a weak CV. Before each application, adjust:

- The summary (two lines matching the role)
- The skills order (mirror the job description's priorities)
- The top bullets under your most relevant experience

This ten-minute step dramatically improves your keyword match and relevance — and skipping it is a major reason [resumes get rejected](/blog/why-resume-rejected) despite strong qualifications.

## Step 8: Format for ATS and Humans

Your beautifully written CV is useless if a parser cannot read it. Follow these:

- Single-column layout, no tables or text boxes
- Standard fonts (Calibri, Arial, Georgia) at 10–12pt
- Consistent dates, bullets, and headings
- One page for freshers, two max for senior roles
- Export as PDF unless Word is requested

These are the foundations of an [ATS-friendly resume](/blog/ats-friendly-resume), and they apply directly to CVs in India.

## Step 9: Proofread and Test

Typos and inconsistencies destroy credibility instantly. After proofreading:

1. Read it aloud to catch awkward phrasing.
2. Check every date and bullet for consistency.
3. **Test it** with a [free AI resume roast](/) — you get an honest score plus a list of weak bullets, vague claims, and formatting issues to fix before any recruiter sees it.

This final test is the difference between *thinking* your CV is good and *knowing* it is.

## How to Write a CV With No Experience

If you have no formal work history, you are not stuck — you reframe. Pull material from:

- Academic and personal projects
- Internships, even virtual or unpaid ones
- Volunteering and college club leadership
- Competitions and self-learning with something to show

Describe these with the same achievement language you would use for a job. A fresher CV that leads with strong projects beats an empty one waiting for "real" experience.

## How to Write a CV: FAQ

### How long should a CV be?

One page for freshers and those with under five years of experience; two pages for senior professionals. True academic curriculum vitae documents can be longer.

### Do I need a different CV for every job?

You need a tailored version. Keep one master CV and adjust the summary, skills, and top bullets per application.

### Should I include hobbies on my CV?

Only if they are relevant or genuinely distinctive. Generic hobbies waste space; a notable one (state-level athlete, published writer) can help.

### What is the most common CV writing mistake?

Listing duties instead of achievements. "Responsible for X" is weak; "Achieved Y by doing X" is strong.
