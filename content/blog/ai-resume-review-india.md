## What Is AI Resume Review?

**AI resume review** means uploading or pasting your CV into a tool that uses large language models to analyze structure, content, keywords, and readability. Unlike basic grammar checkers, modern **resume review AI** systems can:

- Identify vague bullets ("worked on projects" with no detail)
- Flag formatting that breaks Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS)
- Compare your skills section against common job description patterns
- Suggest rewrites for summary and experience sections
- Score your resume and explain the score in plain language

For Indian candidates, the best tools understand context — internships at TCS vs. a college fest project, CGPA presentation, and Hinglish-friendly tone when you want honest feedback without corporate fluff.

One review is not enough. Cycle:

### Step 4: Tailor Per Job (Light Touch)

Keep a master resume. For each application, adjust:
- Summary (2 lines matching role)
- Skills order (mirror job description honestly)
- Top 2 bullets under most relevant experience/project

AI review on the tailored version catches keyword gaps.

## What AI Resume Review Gets Wrong

Do not blindly copy every AI suggestion.

**Over-optimization:** Stuffing keywords you cannot explain in interviews backfires.

**Fabricated metrics:** AI may suggest numbers you do not have. Never lie — use ranges or scope ("supported 50+ daily users") only if true.

**Tone mismatch:** Roast tools are harsh by design. Extract the fix, ignore the drama if it triggers you.

**Industry nuance:** AI may not know your college's tier, local hiring norms, or that "GDSC Lead" matters more than "Class Representative" for tech roles. Use judgment.
