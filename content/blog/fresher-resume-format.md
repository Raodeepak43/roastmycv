Landing your first job in India with no full-time experience feels impossible until you realize recruiters are not looking for ten years of work history — they are looking for potential, clarity, and proof you can deliver. The right **fresher resume format** and a sharp **fresher resume about me** (professional summary) make that visible in six seconds. This guide covers structure, real **Indian fresher CV** examples, common mistakes, and how to build a **resume for freshers** that passes ATS filters and earns interview calls in 2026.

## The Ideal Fresher Resume Structure

Keep your resume one page. Recruiters reviewing campus placement batches read hundreds of CVs — brevity wins.

Recommended section order for Indian freshers:

1. **Header** — Name, phone, email, LinkedIn, city (optional: GitHub for tech roles)
2. **Professional Summary** — 2–3 lines targeting the role type
3. **Education** — Most recent degree first (often your strongest section)
4. **Technical Skills / Skills** — Grouped by category
5. **Projects** — Academic, personal, or internship projects with outcomes
6. **Internships / Training** — If any; otherwise skip and expand projects
7. **Certifications** — Coursera, NPTEL, AWS, etc.
8. **Achievements / Extracurriculars** — Hackathons, competitions, leadership roles

Skip "Objective" statements ("To obtain a challenging position..."). They date your resume instantly. Use a summary instead.

Skip photos, marital status, religion, and full home addresses unless a specific employer requests them — standard for most IT, consulting, and corporate roles in India.

## Common Fresher Resume Mistakes

### Listing skills without proof
"Python, Java, C++, React, Angular, Node, Django, ML, AI, Blockchain" — recruiters assume you know none deeply. List fewer skills you can demo in interviews and back them with projects.

### Weak project descriptions
"Made a website for college" is useless. What stack? What problem? What outcome?

### Copy-paste career objective from the internet
Recruiters have read "seeking a challenging environment to grow" ten thousand times.

### Ignoring ATS rules
Fancy templates fail parsing. Read our [ATS-friendly resume guide](/blog/ats-friendly-resume) before mass applying through company portals.

### Not tailoring for the role
Your base resume stays the same; your summary and project ordering change per application. Lead with the most relevant project for each job.

## How to Fill a No Experience Resume

"No experience" usually means no *full-time* experience. You still have material:

| Source | What to highlight |
|--------|-------------------|
| Academic projects | Tech stack, team size, grade, users |
| Internships | Tasks, tools, metrics, duration |
| Freelance / family business | Clients, revenue, deliverables |
| Open source | Contributions, PRs merged, repos starred |
| Competitions | Rank, problem solved, team role |
| Certifications | Provider, skills validated |
| College roles | Fest organizer, club head, volunteer hours |

Write each entry as a mini job description with action verbs and numbers.

## Formatting Tips Specific to India

- **CGPA vs percentage:** Use whichever is stronger. Many companies ask for both in application forms anyway.
- **10th and 12th marks:** Include if strong (above 85%) and you are early career. Drop them after 2–3 years of experience.
- **Location:** Mention city if targeting specific markets (Bangalore tech, Mumbai finance, Delhi NCR consulting).
- **Languages:** Relevant for customer-facing roles — English plus Hindi or regional language is an asset.
- **Expected CTC:** Never put on resume. Discuss in HR round.

Save as PDF for email applications and .docx when portals request Word format.

## FAQ

### Should freshers keep resume to one page?

Yes, always one page for first job applications. Recruiters expect it. If you have multiple substantial internships and projects, prioritize the strongest four to five entries and cut the rest.

### Is CGPA important on a fresher resume?

Include CGPA if it is 7.0 or above on a 10-point scale (or equivalent). Below that, omit the number and lead with projects and skills instead. Some companies filter by CGPA at campus level — check placement cell cutoffs.

### Can I include school-level achievements?

Include significant achievements (national olympiad, state-level sports, major scholarships) in an Achievements section. Skip minor school prizes unless you have very little else to show — prioritize recent college-level proof.
