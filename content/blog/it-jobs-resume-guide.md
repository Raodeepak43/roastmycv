A strong **it resume** has to work across a huge range of roles — support, systems, networking, cloud, security, and infrastructure — while still feeling specific to the job you want. That is the tricky part. Many candidates applying for IT jobs in India send one generic CV everywhere and wonder why nothing lands. Recruiters at service giants and product companies alike want to see relevant certifications, hands-on skills, and proof you can keep systems running. This guide gives you IT resume examples, a clear structure, and an it cv example framework you can adapt — including a clean entry level it resume path for freshers.

The core principle: an IT resume is judged on reliability and relevance. Show that you fix things, maintain uptime, and know the specific stack the role needs.

## What Recruiters Look For in an IT Resume

When screening for IT jobs, hiring managers scan for:

- **Relevant certifications** (CompTIA, CCNA, AWS, Microsoft, RHCSA, ITIL)
- **Hands-on skills** matching the role — OS, networking, cloud, ticketing tools
- **Evidence of reliability** — uptime, tickets resolved, SLAs met
- **Clear specialisation** rather than a vague "IT generalist" pitch

Freshers should show labs, projects, internships, and certifications. Experienced candidates should show systems managed, scale, and measurable reliability.

## Key Skills to Feature on an IT Resume

Group skills so they scan fast and pass ATS. Tailor the order to the specific IT job.

### Core technical skills (pick what fits the role)

- **Operating systems:** Windows Server, Linux (Ubuntu, RHEL)
- **Networking:** TCP/IP, DNS, DHCP, VPN, firewalls, routing/switching
- **Cloud:** AWS, Azure, or GCP fundamentals
- **Support tools:** ServiceNow, Jira, Active Directory, Office 365 admin
- **Scripting/automation:** PowerShell, Bash, basic Python

### Supporting skills

- Hardware troubleshooting and asset management
- Backup, recovery, and security basics
- ITIL / incident and change management
- Documentation and end-user support

Do not list every technology you have heard of. A focused [resume skills section](/blog/resume-skills-section) reads as competence; an everything-list reads as padding.

## IT Resume Summary Example

Two or three lines: your IT focus, your strongest proof, and key certifications. Avoid "seeking a challenging IT role to utilise my skills."

**For an entry level it resume:**

> IT graduate with CompTIA A+ and hands-on lab experience in Windows Server, Active Directory, and basic networking. Set up a 10-node virtual lab and resolved 50+ practice tickets during an internship. Seeking an entry-level IT support role to grow into systems administration.

**For 2+ years experience:**

> IT systems administrator with 3 years managing Windows and Linux environments for 200+ users. Maintained 99.8% uptime, automated patching with PowerShell, and cut average ticket resolution time by 30%. CCNA and AWS Cloud Practitioner certified.

Both lead with proof. For more patterns, see our [resume summary examples for India](/blog/resume-summary-examples-india).

## Strong Bullet Points (With Metrics)

IT impact is measurable — use it. Apply Action → Context → Result.

1. **Resolved** 40+ support tickets per week with a 95% first-contact resolution rate, improving user satisfaction scores.
2. **Maintained** 99.8% server uptime across 15 production servers through proactive monitoring and patching.
3. **Automated** software deployment with PowerShell scripts, cutting manual setup time per machine from 45 to 10 minutes.
4. **Migrated** 200+ mailboxes to Office 365 with zero data loss and minimal user downtime.
5. **Configured** firewalls and VPN access for a 50-user office, strengthening security with no reported breaches.

Freshers: quantify labs and projects — nodes configured, tickets practised, systems built. Never invent uptime figures. Strengthen bullets with our [resume action verbs](/blog/resume-action-verbs).

## Certifications: A Big Deal in IT

Unlike some fields, certifications genuinely move the needle for IT jobs in India. List them prominently — often right under your summary or in a dedicated section.

- **Entry level:** CompTIA A+, Network+, Microsoft Certified: Fundamentals
- **Networking:** CCNA
- **Cloud:** AWS Cloud Practitioner / Solutions Architect Associate, Azure Fundamentals
- **Linux:** RHCSA, Linux+
- **Service management:** ITIL Foundation

Even one relevant, in-progress certification signals commitment. For freshers, certifications can offset limited experience on an entry level it resume.

## Common IT Resume Mistakes

These quietly cost candidates interviews:

- **One generic CV for every IT role** — support, cloud, and networking need different framing.
- **Listing tools with no context** about what you did with them.
- **No metrics** — "provided IT support" vs. "resolved 40 tickets/week at 95% FCR."
- **Burying or omitting certifications.**
- **Vague soft-skill filler** instead of technical proof.
- **Multi-column templates** that break ATS parsing.

## ATS Keyword Tips for IT Roles

TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL, and most enterprises filter IT applications through ATS. To pass:

- **Mirror the job description:** if it says "Active Directory," "incident management," "AWS," use those exact terms where true.
- **Spell out acronyms once:** "Active Directory (AD)," "Service Level Agreement (SLA)."
- **Include role keywords:** technical support, troubleshooting, networking, cloud computing, system administration, ITIL.
- **List certifications with full names** plus the common acronym.
- **Use a single-column layout** with standard headings and export as PDF.

## Choose a Lane: IT Specialisations

"IT" is too broad to target well. Frame your resume around one path:

- **IT support / helpdesk:** ticketing, troubleshooting, user support, hardware.
- **System administration:** servers, AD, backups, automation.
- **Network engineering:** routing, switching, firewalls, CCNA.
- **Cloud / DevOps:** AWS/Azure, scripting, CI/CD basics.
- **Security:** firewalls, monitoring, compliance.

## Resume Structure That Works

A clean one-page order for most IT jobs (two pages only for senior roles):

1. **Header** — name, city, email, phone, LinkedIn
2. **Summary** — 2–3 lines with proof
3. **Certifications** — prominent, especially for freshers
4. **Skills** — grouped by category
5. **Experience / Internships** — impact bullets
6. **Projects / Labs** — for freshers, above or alongside experience
7. **Education** — degree, year
