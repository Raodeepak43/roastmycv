A **resume maker for freshers** has one hard job: making a candidate with no full-time experience look genuinely hireable. As a fresher, you cannot lean on years of job titles, so your resume has to sell your education, projects, internships, and potential — all on a single, clean page. The good news is that with the right resume builder for freshers and the right structure, you can compete with anyone in your batch, even if your CGPA is average.

This guide is built specifically for Indian freshers: final-year students, recent graduates, and anyone targeting campus placements or their first job in 2026. No fluff, just what works.

## Why Freshers Need a Different Approach

Experienced professionals fill resumes with job history. Freshers do not have that, so copying a senior's CV format is a mistake. A resume for freshers must flip the priorities:

- **Projects over job titles** — your academic and personal projects are your proof
- **Skills you can defend** — not a list of 30 buzzwords
- **Education front and center** — degree, CGPA, relevant coursework
- **Internships and training** — even short ones count if described well

The recruiter knows you are a fresher. They are not expecting 10 years of experience — they are looking for evidence that you can do the job and learn fast. A good resume builder for freshers helps you surface that evidence.

## What Recruiters Actually Look For in a Fresher CV

In India's high-volume hiring, a fresher resume gets 6–10 seconds on the first scan, and only after passing the ATS. Recruiters scan for:

1. **Relevant skills** matching the job description
2. **Project depth** — did you build something real?
3. **Clear education details** — CGPA, branch, year of passing
4. **No red flags** — typos, broken links, photo where prohibited
5. **Clean formatting** that parses correctly

If your CV is a wall of text or a graphics-heavy design, you lose before the content is even read. Start with our [ATS-friendly resume guide](/blog/ats-friendly-resume) to get the structure right.

## How to Create a Resume for Freshers (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Nail the Contact Block

- Full name as per official documents
- Indian mobile number
- Professional email
- LinkedIn URL (updated and clean)
- GitHub or portfolio (essential for tech and design roles)
- City and state only

### Step 2: Write a 2-Line Summary

Freshers should replace the old "objective" with a sharp summary stating degree, branch, and one strong proof point. Example: "B.Tech IT 2026 (CGPA 7.8) with two full-stack projects and a 2-month web development internship. Seeking software developer roles." See more [resume summary examples for India](/blog/resume-summary-examples-india).

### Step 3: Lead With Education

For each qualification:

- Degree, branch, college name
- CGPA (if 7.0+) or percentage
- Year of passing
- 10th and 12th marks if applying to service companies

Do not hide a low CGPA — HR verifies it. Compensate with strong projects instead.

### Step 4: Build a Strong Projects Section

This is where freshers win or lose. Include at least two projects, each with:

- A clear title (link if it is live)
- The tech stack used
- Two or three bullets covering the problem, your role, and the outcome

Weak: "Made a weather app." Strong: "Built a weather app (React, OpenWeather API) showing 5-day forecasts for 100+ Indian cities, with location auto-detect." Specificity sells.

### Step 5: Add Internships and Training

Even a one-month virtual internship counts. List the company, duration, and two bullets describing real deliverables. No internship? Lean harder on projects, hackathons, and open-source contributions.

### Step 6: Group Your Skills Honestly

List 8–12 core skills grouped by category (languages, web, databases, tools). Only include what you can defend in an interview — listed skills get tested.

### Step 7: Add Achievements and Certifications

Hackathon ranks, relevant certifications (NPTEL, AWS, Google), and leadership tied to outcomes. Skip irrelevant filler.

## Fresher Resume Mistakes That Cause Instant Rejection

- **"Skills: MS Office, Internet, Hard Working"** — meaningless filler
- **Photo on IT company applications** — often prohibited
- **Listing 15 programming languages** — interview disaster
- **Identical project descriptions** copied from batchmates — plagiarism checks exist
- **Broken GitHub links** — 404 repos destroy credibility
- **Two pages** when one clean page is stronger
- **Generic objective** like "seeking a challenging role in a reputed organization"

## FAQ

### How do I make a resume with no experience?

Lead with education, then a strong projects section, then any internships or training. Projects are your proof of skill when you lack job history. Use action verbs and measurable outcomes.

### Is there a free resume maker for freshers in India?

Yes. MyCVRoast offers a free builder with no download paywall, plus AI feedback. Always verify that a tool's "free" claim includes free export.

### One page or two pages for a fresher resume?

One page. Without full-time experience, you rarely need two. A focused single page is stronger than a padded two-pager.

### Should freshers include 10th and 12th marks?

Yes for service companies (TCS, Infosys, Wipro), which often require them. For product companies, projects and skills matter more, but including marks does no harm.

### How many projects should a fresher list?

At least two strong projects with metrics. Quality over quantity — two well-described projects beat five vague ones.
