## What Is an ATS and Why Does It Reject You?

An Applicant Tracking System is software that collects, parses, and ranks resumes. When you apply online, the ATS:

1. **Parses** your resume into structured data (name, skills, experience)
2. **Scores** it against the job description for keyword match
3. **Ranks** candidates so recruiters review the top matches first

If the ATS cannot parse your resume — because of tables, columns, images, or unusual fonts — your information gets scrambled or lost. A scrambled resume scores low and never reaches a human. ## ATS Formatting Rules (Non-Negotiable)

A free ATS resume builder should enforce these automatically, but understand them yourself:

- **Single-column layout** — multi-column confuses parsing order
- **Standard section headings** — "Work Experience," "Education," "Skills"
- **No tables or text boxes** — content inside them often gets dropped
- **No images, logos, or photos** — ATS cannot read them
- **Standard fonts** — Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman
- **Simple bullet points** — not custom symbols
- **PDF or .docx** as specified by the portal
- **No headers/footers** for critical info — some ATS skip them

Pretty does not equal parseable. Clarity wins.

## How ATS Keywords Work

The ATS matches your resume against the job description. The closer your wording, the higher you rank. To use ATS keywords effectively:

### Mirror the Job Description

If the posting says "React.js," do not just write "React" — include the exact phrasing where honest. If it asks for "data analysis," use that phrase, not only "analytics."

### Include Both Spelled-Out and Acronym Forms

Write "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" so you match either form the ATS looks for.

### Place Keywords in Context

Do not dump a keyword list. Weave skills into your bullets: "Built REST APIs using Node.js and Express, serving 1,000+ daily requests." This satisfies both the ATS and the human reader.

### Never Stuff Keywords

Cramming skills you cannot defend backfires — in interviews and sometimes in ATS spam filters. Be honest. For more on what fails, read [why resumes get rejected](/blog/why-resume-rejected).

## How to Build a Free ATS Resume (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Start With an ATS-Safe Template

Use a single-column, text-based template. Our [resume builder](/resume-builder) produces ATS-friendly output by default, so you do not have to fight formatting.

### Step 2: Structure Your Sections Clearly

In order: contact block, summary, skills, experience/projects, education, certifications. Use standard headings the ATS recognizes.

### Step 3: Add a Skills Section With Relevant Keywords

Group 8–12 core skills that match the job description. This is the densest keyword zone — make it count, but stay honest.

### Step 4: Write Achievement-Based Bullets

Each bullet: action verb + what you did + measurable result. This naturally embeds keywords while proving impact.

### Step 5: Tailor Per Job

Keep a master resume, then adjust the summary, skills order, and top bullets for each application to match its keywords. Generic CVs rank lower.

## Common ATS Mistakes to Avoid

- **Using a two-column "modern" template** that scrambles in parsing
- **Putting your name or contact info only in the header** — some ATS skip it
- **Saving as an image-based PDF** — text must be selectable
- **Creative section titles** like "My Journey" instead of "Experience"
- **Keyword stuffing** that reads unnaturally
- **Skipping the ATS check** before applying
